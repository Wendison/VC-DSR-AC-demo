VC performance of different methods

  • System comparison:
    • Original: Original dysarthric or L2 speech.
    • E2E-VC: End-to-end VC for non-standard speech.
    • Enc-CM (proposed): Multi-speaker conversion model by using the speaker encoding to control the speaker identity of the converted speech.
    • Ada-CM (proposed): Multi-speaker conversion model by using the learned speaker embedding to control the speaker identity of the converted speech.
  • Two speakers are used for experiments:
    • M07: a dysarthric speaker has moderate severe dysarthria with the speech having low intelligibility, selected from UASpeech dataset.
    • LXC: a non-native English speaker with obvious Chinese accent, selected from L2-ARCTIC dataset.
  • Additional observations:
    • Speaker encoder may produce insuffient speaker embeddings to capture speaker characteristics, especially for dysarthric speech, the gender of some converted voice is even changed, please listen to M07-No.5 and M07-No.7 of E2E-VC, and M07-No.4 of Enc-CM for more details. On the contrary, the proposed Ada-CM can obtain effective speaker embeddings via speaker adaptation to generate the speech with higher speaker similarity.
