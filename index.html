Audio samples: Improved Voice conversion for dysarthric and L2 speech

Authors: Disong Wang, Lifa Sun, Xixin Wu, Xunying Liu and Helen Meng

Abstract: Though significant progress has been achieved for the voice conversion (VC) of standard speech, VC for non-standard speech, e.g., dysarthric and second-language (L2) speech, is still challenging. Motivated by our previous work applying the end-to-end VC for tackling the dysarthric speech reconstruction and accent conversion tasks, we propose an improved VC system. First, a speech-encoder strives to extract robust phoneme embeddings from the non-standard speech. Second, the phoneme duration and logarithmic fundamental frequency (lf0) predictors are introduced to predict standard phoneme durations and lf0 based on the phoneme embeddings. Third, a multi-speaker conversion model takes the predicted standard lf0 and phoneme embeddings expanded by predicted standard durations as inputs to generate the standard speech, conditioned on the target speaker embedding. Due to the explicit modelling of VC process, speaker encoding and adaptation can be performed to control the speaker identity of the converted speech with high naturalness and intelligibility. Extensive experiments verify the effectiveness of the proposed methods to significantly reduce the dysarthric and non-native-accented pronunciation patterns for dysarthric and L2 speakers, respectively.

VC performance of different methods

  • System comparison:
    • Original: Original dysarthric or L2 speech.
    • E2E-VC: End-to-end VC for non-standard speech.
    • SE-CM (proposed): Multi-speaker conversion model by using the speaker encoding to control the speaker identity of the converted speech.
    • SA-CM (proposed): Multi-speaker conversion model by using the learned speaker embedding to control the speaker identity of the converted speech.
  • Two speakers are used for experiments:
    • M07: a dysarthric speaker has moderate severe dysarthria with the speech having low intelligibility, selected from UASpeech dataset.
    • LXC: a non-native English speaker with obvious Chinese accent, selected from L2-ARCTIC dataset.
